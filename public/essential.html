Essential

AI-powered local delivery that keeps your kitchen stocked with the essentials.

Overview

Essential is AI-powered grocery delivery service that automatically estimates food consumption and order foods that would be used up soon. My design team created this concept for an internal competition at ID sponsored by Google Waymo.

We considered not only UX/UI design but also dataset, backside system and how we would leverage AI to solve complex problems.

Problem Framing

When we talk about grocery store shopping, keeping essentials stocked at home accounts for most time spent grocery shopping. But it’s the least enjoyable part of the shopping experience.

Most of the time is tedious and boring: grabbing the essential necessities

“It's boring to spend most of your time in the grocery store picking up the same things from the same shelves every time.” --- Jesse

Few of the time is delightful: exploring new foods at the grocery store

“Exploring new items in the grocery store with your family is a ritual that makes life more interesting.” ---- Hugo

User Pain Points

At home, it’s hard to…

  • Control different consumption rate of essentials
  • Remember what items have already run out
  • Planning when and how much to stock up
  • Control total budget with all food channels
  • Staying aware of shelf life for perishables

Online, it’s hard to…

  • Compare attributes (i.e.nutrition, price) of different items
  • Remember what items have already run out
  • Be aware of the delivery time to avoid parcels being stolen from the door
  • Always get the right item and brand

At store, it’s hard to…

  • Find parking spot and carrying tons of stuff
  • Go to same aisles to get same thing as always
  • Calculate price with physical shopping cart
  • Balance overall nutrition
  • Minimize time spent at checkout

User Needs

Predict food amount

Delivery just-in-time

Eat healthier at home

Control spending on foods

How might we predict how much the user uses up their foods and figure out how often we should deliver new foods?

How might we deliver essential foods on the right time when the user is available and feels convenient?

How might we encourage the user to replace foods with more healthier ones that are customized to each user health condition?

How might we support the user to manage spending on foods which is difficult to see as a whole?

Solution

Quick Onboarding

To set-up AI model for a better service --- food consumption rate and available time schedule are critical. The app caluculates them by getting 4 kinds of data, household structure, eating habits, purchase history, and connections with smart home devices.

Ask the user to input household structure, which is the most direct factor that determines your food consumption rate

Get frequency to eat outside or order foods to help us better adjust our recommendation frequency

Connect with other applications like Wholefoods to share your purchase history, or use uploaded image of fridge contents to understand how much the user eats and store foods

Connect with IoT like Google Nest can tell when the user is at home to deliver foods on time

Perfect Quantity Prediction

Based on food consumption data, the service delivers essential foods by perfect amount and appropriate frequency

On home screen, the user can check essentials’ status including incoming delivery, current remaining essentials, last week’s purchase history, and future consumption estimates.

The auto-generated list of personalized recommended grocery items, based on the data collected from order history, food consumption rate, and personal preferences. It can be manually adjusted the quantity, which will be helpful feedback to tune AI model.

Just-in-Time Delivery

When the user is going to check out, according to available time information from IoT devices, the app automatically recommends possibile delivery time for convenience.

Besides the automated delivery time, the app also provides quick access to manually adjustment which is also a co-learning interaction between you and our model

When a delivery is on the way, the user would be notified and can adjust delivery time, address, and even cancel the order

Healthier Substitution

In addition to those 2 functionalities above, the app recommends healthier food substitutions based on the user input of basic health conditions, and food preferences that help the app create biometrics and diet profiles

Show daily health conditions, food concerns and preferences when the user agrees to turn on the function

A healthier food option is shown on a recommended shopping list. The user can check nutrition details of recommended foods

Prudent Budget Management

It is activated when the user allow to connect with a budget management app. Essential focuses on how much the user spends on grocery shopping, eating outside, and ordering foods to help the user manage a total expenditure

Not only show expenditure, but also provide spending in similar household structures for reference

Backend System

To provide 4 main functionalies, the app collects the required data that are relevant to each feature. For food prediction and healtheir substitutions that need more complicated judgement, raters label the datasets before passing AI to give criteria of subjective assesment. AI or program outputs recommendations to the user, and then it would get feedback to improve precision level of its prediction

Food Quantity Dataset

Choose food quantitiy dataset as one of the instances for explaing the system more in details. To calculate food consumption rate, the machine learning model needs 3 data. First one is purchase history, when, how many and how much the buys each food. The model can mathematically predict when the foods would be depleted.

For example, for user A, the model can predict he buys one pack of eggs every 7days on average, based on the purchase history from Amazon.

Data Source

  • Purchase history from Amazon
  • Quantitative survey of 10,000 households across the U.S.
  • Qualititative research - observing different types of familes

The second data is food storage and consumption. From user input or a smart refrigerator, food quantity, food weight change on the fridge shelf and frequency of ordering foods and eating outside are collected. Based on them, rators judge whether the food strage would last for one week that is very difficult for the model to assess.

Data Source

  • User input
  • Partnering with Samsung to detect when items in the refrigerator have been depleted
  • Weight data from the refrigerator or food containers to see how fast foods are eaten

The model utilizes images in a fridge that come from smart fridges or pictures that our user takes, because image recognition is one of what AI is good at. Even though here is a problem that ituations in the fridge are very different in each household. As you can see in the 2 images on the slide, some people prefer to preserve foods in a container while others do not. The raters teach which foods are stored and how enough they are, considering your household structures.
