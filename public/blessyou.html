Bless You!

Who it's for

Bless You! is designed for action game fans who may love to play Super Mario for Donkey Kong. In addition, this game targets at people who like indie games that have simple game mechanism and consists of pixel arts rather than high-end games

Game Concept

Story

Superheros are always busy. They never stop fighting against villain to save people. Yet, they are also humans whereas they have superpower. They are beyond fatigue and extremely exhausted due to never ending patrol activities. One day, one of the superheroes gets a sick because of the chronic fatigue. His superpower is restricted to high jump only when he sneezes.

However, he must keep working to save people who need his help. Villains will not wait for him until he recovers from the illness. Now, he gets a call to rescue kidnapped citizens who is tied up on the rooftop of tall buildings. Villains set a trap to kill him.

He cannot lose this challenge...

Game Mechanics

The player requires to go up to a top of each stage to save a kidnapped child from a villain. There are traps such as spike that damage the hero's life. The player has to avoid touching the traps 3 times.

On the left top on the screen, there is a life gauge and a sneeze bar whose mecanism will be explained later.

The sneeze bar endlessly goes to zero and back to full every 2 seconds. When it reaches zero, the hero does "Sneeze Jump" because he cannot control his power appropriately.

The sneeze jump enables the hero to go up to a higher place when he is at the middle of normal jump, which gives the player a strategy to consider when to jump.

Interaction

The player can move the hero with "W" -- Normal Jump, "A" -- Move Left, and "D" -- Move Right. Arrow keys are also available.

I chose pixel art because not only I love the art as I have played video games since my childhood, but also I though that the player would focus on game playing. Seeking the right timing of normal jump requires concentration.

Background

I created the game background with a tile set because it was easy to use patterns. The tile set was made with aseprite, and the tile map was created with Tiled map editor.

Game level was taken into consideration when I made the background. I tried not making the stage too difficult since it would be the first stage. Also, I intended to put the elements to get the player understand the basic game mechanism and how to play.

Development

I used Phaser, HTML5 game framework for desktop and mobile, because it was suitable to create a pixel game.

Below is a file structure that I applied a module style to keep it simple and easy to maintenance. The Phaser library is imported into index.html. The visual assets are preloaded in preloarder.js, animations are created in playerjs, and they are imported into game.js, a main JS file to add physics or make animation functions.

Double Jump Function

Create a time event which causes "Sneeze Jump" every time the sneeze bar reaches zero

Code a function of "Sneeze Jump" that add a physical effect, and play its animation and sound

Use conditionals and "currentPlayerAnim" in a code of basic actions to differenciate the normal jump animation from that of sneeze jump

Decrease Life Guage Function

It was also challenging to make a function that would decrease the number of the hearts when the player touches the spike, which required me to understand the data structure. With "console.log", I detected a sprite of each heart stored in an array in a children object in the heart object.

After detecting the location, I used "getChildren()" to reach to the array and delete one of the heart elements. I also used a conditional to restart the game when the array becomes empty.
