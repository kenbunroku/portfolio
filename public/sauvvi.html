SAUVVI

Semi-Autonomous Vehicle Visual Interface for energy efficient driving

Overview

SAUVVI is a project funded by IIT / WISER Cross-Diciplinary Seed Funding Grants that aims to explore, create and test limited instructional cues visually presented on the display of such semi-autonomous vehicles.

Through the information and interaction design on display, we tried to intervene two diver actions, speed acceleration/deceleration and lane change, to encourage energy efficient driving.

Time

Jan.2020 ~ May.2020

(User test has been pending due to COVID)

Industrial Research

My job was to educate the team members who were not familiar with an automotive industry about technological trends. First of all, I visited CES 2020 to research the latest information display that were planned to introduce in the market next 5~10 years. I recorded videos, took pictures and experienced UX / UI of automotive manufacturers, suppliers and tech companies.

Analysis Report

After the research, I analyzed the trend and summarized the information severating hardware and interaction to help the members understand factor by factor.

For the time line, even though not all the companies did not mention which year they were going to introduce the new technologies and designs, I synsethized my knowledge in the automotive industry to assume when they would seem to launch them.

After going through the abstract trends structually, I introduced design and interactions of each company as concrete examples by picture and video.

Automation Levels of Autonomous Vehicle

The other task was to educate what "Semi-Autonomous Vehicle" meant in the industry because there is a range of automation levels of autonomous vehicle.

I lectured the definition with a material from National Hightway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) which is one of the most authorized explanations.

Source: NHTSA
